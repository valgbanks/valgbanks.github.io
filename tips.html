What I'm Learning

This page is a collection of small tips that help me when I'm taking photos. I'm still learning, so these aren't “perfect rules”—just things that make practicing feel easier and more enjoyable.

Quick Tips

  • Start with light: Soft natural light (like near a window) can make photos look instantly better.
  • Move first: Step closer, back up, or change your angle before adjusting camera settings.
  • Take a few shots: I usually take multiple photos and compare what worked best.

Practice Cheat Sheet

A simple table of what I usually try depending on the situation.

When I'm shooting… I usually try… So the photo feels…
In low light Raising ISO a little Brighter (without using flash)
Something is moving A faster shutter speed Less blurry and more clear
Portraits A lower aperture (if possible) Softer in the background

I'm still practicing these, but this helps me remember what to try first.
